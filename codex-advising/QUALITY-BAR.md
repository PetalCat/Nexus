# Codex Research Quality Bar

Codex should help Claude by improving confidence, clarity, and repo awareness.

Good findings usually include:

- a precise question
- whether the work is local, online, or both
- named sources with links when external research is used
- a distinction between sourced facts and inference
- a distinction between local repo evidence and outside-world evidence
- why the result matters for Nexus
- open questions or uncertainty

## Preferred Shape

For most research-heavy notes, prefer sections like:

1. Question or Goal
2. Local Evidence
3. External Sources
4. Key Facts
5. Inference / Advisory Takeaway
6. Relevance To Nexus
7. Open Questions

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid findings that are mostly:

- shallow summaries with no evidence
- rigid implementation mandates
- conclusions that do not distinguish repo facts from outside research
- advice that ignores current local code or issue context

Claude may change this quality bar whenever a better system is more useful.
